Description

  • CS 6741 is a topics class on the intersection of natural language processing and machine learning with a rotating focus and style. The theme of this class this semester is Discrete Variables / Deep Learning. The course will have three (discrete) parts.

    1. A modern treatment of discrete variable models and structured prediction for several classic problems in NLP. (Lecture-Based)
    2. A survey of current tasks and challenges in contermporary NLP and a collaborative discussion of potential research problems. (Teacher/Student-presented)
    3. Project workshopping and group feedback. (Open-form)

Preliminaries
  • This course is not meant to be a comprehensive treatment of NLP or deep learning. For NLP, I will assume a working knowledge of basic problems such as classification and language modeling. For deep learning, I will assume knowledge of PyTorch and the ability to quickly pick-up deep learning models from tutorials.

Project

  • The goal of this class is to produce original research in natural language processing or machine learning. As such, we will work towards the goal of submitting a paper to a top-tier conference. You should aim to submit an abstract half-way through the class and a final project by the end of the term.
